VERGILIUS
Holland's remarkable study includes Vergil in its intriguing compass. She
shows that Lucretius came
from
the same area of Northern
Italy
the
Transpadane - as Catullus and Vergil. She examines language-attitudes and
peculiarities of the period, the historical relations between poets from the
provinces and the urban audience in Rome, and the personal and political
connections of literary figures of the period. She also argues that the original
dedication of De Rerum Natura was to Julius Caesar.

Caltabiano argues that Augustus tried
to erase negative aspects of the
tradition attaching to Marcellus. Coelius Antipater (and Livy) recounted the
impiety of the consul (and the defection of the Etruscans). Vergil, Propertius,
and Valerius Maximus reflect a revised tradition attaching to M. Claudius
Marcellus.
