The Georgics remain the neglected masterpiece in recent Vergilian scholarship. Most
significant and directional are Frentz's study (22) of the mythological element in
the poems, a major contribution to a fundamental aspect, and Wilkinson's preview
(24) of a major study of the Georgics. Wilkinson's remarks on the failure of the
ancients to adopt mountain-climbing for pleasure needs the corrective of a neglected
article by A.S. Pease, 'Notes on Mountain Climbing in Antiquity' in Appalachia33
(1961) 289-298. Mountford (23) lectures on the plan and arrangement of Vergil's
material in relation to his dominating purposes. Form, balance, and coherent design
are his proper concern.
