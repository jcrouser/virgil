VERGIL AND THE ARTS
Bensch, Matthias. 2023. Held im Bild: Zur visuellen Konstruktion heroischer
Figuren im Imperium Romanum anhand von Darstellungen des Aeneas
und des Romulus. Rahden.
Hallett, Christopher H. 2023. “The Role of References to the Visual Arts in
Vergil’s Aeneid.” Maia 75: 233–50.
Pergola, Agnese, and Chiara Giobbe. 2023. “L’Eneide rappresentata nelle
raccolte del Museo Nazionale Romano.” Maia 75: 278–96.
Sauron, Gilles. 2023. “Virgilio e l’arte greca: Iconografia, stile,
composizione.” Maia 75: 251–64.
Hallett notes that the exegesis of some passages in the Aeneid (2.707–
25 and 7.630–34) does not presuppose an allusion to other texts, but to
artistic images. Sauron shows that Vergil’s ekphrases in Ecl. 3.35–48 and
Aen. 8.671–713 reveal his precise knowledge of Greek art and its different
styles. Pergola and Giobbe analyze artworks dating to the first century
CE and created for private use, now housed in the Museo Nazionale
Romano, that offer representations of myths of Rome’s origin or refer to
Vergil’s Aeneid. Comparison with pieces datable to a period preceding
the poem’s composition shows that, when the iconographic tradition had
not yet consolidated around standard forms, artists turned to the literary
tradition, which was rich in figurative details.
