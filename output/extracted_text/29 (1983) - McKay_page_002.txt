VERGILIAN BIBLIOGRAPHY
1982-83
The present survey of Vergilian scholarship is the twenty-first
in the Vergilius series. Classical
World 68:1 (1974) published in association with The
Vergilian Society, Inc., contains my 'Recent Work on Vergil: A Bibliographical Survey, 1964-73'. The survey was reprinted in The Classical World
Bibliography of Vergil (Walter Donlan, ed.), Garland Publishing, Inc. (New
York & London 1978). Subsequent reviews have appeared in Vergilius 19
(1973), 20 (1974), 21 (1975), 22 (1976), 23 (1977), 24 (1978), 25 (1979),
26 (1980), 27 (1981), and 28 (1982).
The Bimillenary celebrations have given rise to a relentless stream of commentary, criticism, exposition and exhibition. Several journals
Gymnasium
90 (1983), Helmantica
33 (1982), Listy Filologické (Symposium Vergilianum: 16th International Eirene Conference) 106 (1983); Omnibus 3 (1982);
Würzburger Jahrbucher für die Altertumswissenschaft 8 (1982) have highlighted Vergilian material and the parade of exhibition catalogues provides a
marvelous perspective of Vergiliana in public and private collections.
The present compilation is indebted to the assistance and cooperation of
Mills Memorial Library of McMaster University, to the Robarts Library of the
University of Toronto, to the library of the Institute of Classical Studies and
Hellenic and Roman Societies, and to the Library of the University of London. Offprints of articles and bibliographic notices for inclusion in the annual
Vergilius review were received abundantly and scholars are once again asked
to send material to the editor of Vergilian Bibliography, c/o Department of
Classics, McMaster University, Hamilton, Ontario, Canada L8S 4M2.
