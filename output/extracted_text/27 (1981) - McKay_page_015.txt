Poehlmann argues that the Hadrumetum (Sousse) mosaic derives from an
Augustan original. The juxtaposition of the poet with Clio and Melpomene
reflects Hellenistic and Imperial theories of poetry's relationship to history
and tragedy. Rawson's article suggests that Q. Cornificius, involved with the
Catullian circle, may have been a friend of Vergil who would have favoured
his De Etymis Deorum.

Bolz examines a manuscript of the Georgics at Gniezno, supplies a text
and an abbreviated commentary, along with a close study of the final verses
of Geo. 1. Krochalis explores a recently acquired partial Aeneid manuscript at
Princeton's Firestone Library. It is the only extant Vergil ms. written not in
book form but on a roll of unusual dimensions: 2!4 inches across, 8V2feet
long! The ms. (ca. 1400-1425) is the work of an Italian scribe experimenting
with a new script (there are Gothic survivals) and an old format.
