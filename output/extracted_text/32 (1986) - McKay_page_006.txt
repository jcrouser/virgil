Tandy offers a fresh assessment of ingredere ( G . 1.42): "enter (your
temple)," or "cross the threshold." Harbinson detects the short-toed eagle (Circaetus
gallicus) rather than the white stork (ciconia) in Vergil's white bird (Č7. 2.319-20).
Landolfi treats Vergil's negative view of love and in particular the sphragis of his
attitude in the celebrated excursus ( G . 3.242-83). Makowski detects playful wit in
the juxtaposition of Maecenas with haud mollia iussa , thereby accenting a salient
feature of his patron's persona. Abramowicz regards the Aristaeus epyllion as a
replacement for the eulogy of Gallus which was censored by Augustus. Schwartz,
inter alia , associates G. 4 and Tibullus 1.7 and underscores their pervasive concern
with poetry's ability to confer immortality.
