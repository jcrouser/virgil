Coarelli states that the sculptures found in the cave at Sperlonga have no
relation with Vergil's epic. Geyer notes Nero's preference for Trojan War
stories and suggests that the Laocoon sculpture, lodged inthe Domus Aurea,
suited his 'classical' orientation. Goodfellow's thesis collects all Transpadane
references, direct and indirect, in order to reassess the part played by boyhood memories inVergil's poetry. Peculiarities of Transpadane flora, fauna,
agricultural procedures, legends and natural phenomena are included in the
study. Ogilvie's review of Hampe's Sperlonga und Vergil (Mainz 1972) corrects improper arguments and associations. Pearsall and Salter devote Chapter
I (pages 3-24) to Classical Traditions, and include Vergil's Italy in their compass. Säflund assigns the 'Germanicus' statue by Cleômenes to M. Claudius
Marcellus, heroised as Hermes Chthonios, and regards it as a posthumous
portrait inspired by Vergil's poetic vision inAeneld 6. Tilly grapples with the
precise location of Fescennium (cf. Aeneid 7. 695-7) and favours Narce, 10
kilometres south of Cività Castellana over Corchiano. Winner argues that the
Laocoon reconstruction was based on Vergil and Pliny.

concentrates on the wisdom-tradition (rather than on the vegetation tradition) and emphasizes Vergil's originality inhis use of the theme. Underworld

irony. Johnston's forthcoming article shows how Vergil's conception of Saturnus evolves from a rough equivalent of Hesiod's Cronus inthe

Aeneid 8. Walsh examines the concept of hope with emphasis on Augustan
writers, Apuleius, and Paulinus of Nola.
