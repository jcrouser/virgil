Basson examines the concept of peace in connection with the Golden Age
in Vergil's works. Coleman argues that the gods of the Hellenized literaryreligious tradition were not capable of bearing the serious burden of causation
that Vergil placed upon them. Den Adel counters Fontenrose's (1978) statement that 'there was no oracle' at Delos. Vergil, and most Greek and Roman
authors did believe in some prophecy there, although there are no extant
records of oracular procedures and no quotable utterances. Certainly Vergil
credits Delos with mantic activity (Aen. 3.92-6). Grimal's provocative article
examines Epicurean traits in the Georgies. Schilling's lecture provides a global
view of Vergil's work, commending his mastery of form and the serious tenor
of his philosophical thought.
