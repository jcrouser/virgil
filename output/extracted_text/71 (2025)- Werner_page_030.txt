EIGHTEENTH CENTURY
Ribeiro Leite, Leni, and Dreykon Fernandes Nascimento. 2025. “Uses of
the Aeneid in O Uraguay (1769), by Basílio da Gama, as Indices of
uariatio between the Epic and the Tragic.” CRJ 17: 93–111.
Despite the epic unity of the poem O Uraguay by the Portuguese-Brazilian
poet Basílio da Gama (1740–1795), Ribeiro Leite and Fernandes Nascimento
detect continuous exchanges between epic and tragic, a characteristic
influenced by the poem’s model, the Aeneid, and a tool used also by Gama
for political commentary.
NINETEENTH CENTURY
Vallana, Fulvio. 2023. “Looking at Infinity, Looking at the Sky: Virgil’s
Eclogues and Leopardi’s Bucolic Poems.” PVS 31: 147–71.
———. 2025. “Virgilian Heroism(s) in Giacomo Leopardi’s All’Italia.” IJCT
32: 121–41.
Vallana (2023) observes that it is not easy to find textual allusions to the
Eclogues in the Idylls by the Italian poet Giacomo Leopardi (1798–1837).
But the fact that the poems are called Idylls, by their author’s choice, asks
us to consider them in their bucolic dimension and to investigate how far
their forms and contents are indebted to the tradition of bucolic poetry and
how much can be attributed instead to Leopardi’s original interpretation
of bucolic. Vallana (2025) examines Vergilian echoes in Leopardi’s poem
All’Italia, focusing on how he drew on heroic figures from the Aeneid to
shape his vision of modern heroism.
