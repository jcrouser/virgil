The Eclogues are studied for structure, cryptograms and the like. Acrostichs,
mesostichs and telestichs are part and parcel of Latin poetry, but the 'clues'
can lead to unlikely assertions: e.g., that the Aeneid was dedicated to Antonius Sosius! Schmid offers a wealth of information and suggestion for the
wary. Willcock's study of battle conventions, the presentation of a warrior's
aristeia, and the identification of minor warriors is highly serviceable and
enlightening. Deryck Williams' Sussex lecture interprets the main themes of
the epic and concludes that the poem is an exploration of contrasting viewpoints of human life. T. R. Glover's phrase, There is a wavering about the
poem* seems particularly apt in light of modern criticism of the epic. Wiltshire's article detects a tension between public and private realms in the
Aeneid that is maintained and eased by the capacity of some characters to act
in the public realm at a distance from the self. Courage, truthfulness (devotion and piety) help to preserve the public realm ;self-distancing is an essential
virtue. Her bimillenary address constructs an intellectual framework for a
reading of Vergil in our time.
