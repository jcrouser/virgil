Beck’s book gives an account of the “simile world” created across
Greek and Latin epic poetry over the centuries, including discussion of
Homer, Apollonius Rhodius, Vergil, and Ovid. García Domingo discusses
Vergil’s linguistic variations and innovations of personal names based
on the forms Thy(m)band Θύ(μ)βρ-. Kennedy discusses the function
of metalepsis in the work of Plato, Vergil, and other ancient texts. Silva
discovers a telestich in Aen. 8.246–49 that presents the Greek word σῆμα
(“portent, wonder, tomb”). The word is significant from its context (the
battle between Hercules and Cacus), with the insistence in the Latin
poetic tradition on the imagery of light and revelation and the use of the
phrase mirabile dictu (8.252), which appears in the same episode. Tamás
finds the hidden signature Ma(ro)–Pu(blius)–Ve(rgilius) in Vergil’s vatic
announcement maius opus moueo at Aen. 7.45.
