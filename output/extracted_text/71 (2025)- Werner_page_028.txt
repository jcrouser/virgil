Abbamonte and Stok demonstrate that Pomponio Leto (1428–1498)
made use of technical works of classical literature (including Strabo
for geography, Theophrastus’s works on plants, Columella, Varro, and
others), to inaugurate a new way of interpreting the Georgics. Aurelio
Brandolini, who was probably in contact with Leto during his stay in
Rome (ca. 1480–1490), wrote an unpublished commentary on the Georgics,
preserved in the Bibliotheca Apostolica Vaticana, MS Vat. lat. 2740,
