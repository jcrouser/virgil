Religion and philosophy tend to be somewhat undernourished in Vergilian
scholarship. Some new advances and prospects have emerged in studies by Bianco,
Glücklich, and Quiter. Orphism and Pythagorean doctrine and practice claim their
due. Della Corte and Fasciano are concerned with Destiny, with Juno as protector of
Greece and Carthage, and with Venus as Aeneadum genetrix. Quiter's concern with
ritual aspects of Aeneas' descent excites fresh interest in the initiatory pattern of the
catabasis. Quiter favors the tunnel at Baiae over the Cumaean grotto as inspirational
source for Vergil, but the date is contested and the use obscure.
