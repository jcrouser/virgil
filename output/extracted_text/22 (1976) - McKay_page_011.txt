The papyrus collection of the Egyptian Museum at Charlottenburg has
disclosed fragments of a papyrus Codex containing a Latin-Greek text of the
Aeneid. There are fragments from Aeneid 1 (notably 21 1-232, 276-297) and
the beginning of Aeneid 2. The text appears inslender double columns which
contain
not verses but single words,
or groups of words, with the Greek
cursive version facing the Latin. The handwriting style dates ca. 350-380 A.D.
"Der Kodex gehört zu den ältesten enthaltenen Vergilhandschriften." The
Aeneid was translated into Greek by the iibertus Polybius during the first
century A.D. The West Berlin Tusculum' (or Loeb) version must have been
designed for teachers (grammatici) or for general school use inthe Eastern
Roman Empire.
