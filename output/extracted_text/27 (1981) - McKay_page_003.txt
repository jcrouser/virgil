16. John Van Sickle, 'The Bookroll and Some Conventions of the Poetic
Book,' Arethusa 13 (1980) 5-42.
Alpers' version of the pastoral poems continues to win supporters. Van
Sickle has adopted a combative position vis-á-vis unfavourable criticism and
has challenged DuQuesnay's reflective critique inthe Liverpool monthly. Van
Sickle's argument, that the Eclogues form an integrated design, together with
his arguments regarding the composition and arrangement of Roman poetry
books, attains a high-water mark inVergilian criticism.
