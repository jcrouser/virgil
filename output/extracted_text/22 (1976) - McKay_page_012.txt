(1975).
132. David Thompson, Dante's Epic Journey (Baltimore, 1974). Rev. C.
Kopff,C/71 (1975-6) 281.
133. O. Zwierlein, 'Karolus Magnus - alter Aeneas/ Lit und Sprache im.
europ. MA. Festschr. K. Langosch. (Darmstadt 1973) 44-52.
Michael Ayrton, artist and sculptor, profoundly affected by the Maze and
the Minotaur, delivered a remarkably original Memorial Lecture. His death in
1975 removed a great talent and
rich
testimony to Vergil's and Cumae's
pervasive influence. Currie assesses the poetry of Vergil as a major influence
on St. Augustine's mind and spirit. The Gibaldi-LaFleur artick deals with
Dante's use of Vergil's Laocoon episode (as explained by Servius)
in hi*
depiction of Vanni Fucci (Inferno 24-25). Gransden (48) issues a valuable
summons: 'The best way to go on encouraging people to get something out of
the Aeneid
is to read it -
at least sometimes and at least some of it -
alongside passages from Spenser's Faerie Queene and Milton's Paradise Lost,
vernacular masterpieces produced by men who were the products of a culture
in which the Aeneid was not merely accessible but central.' Huxley's entertaining note treats Girolamo Fracastoro's De Morbo Gallico (Verona, 1530), a
parody of Vergilian construction and digression. Wilson Knight's biography of
his classicist brother
is richly
informative and a worthy memorial. Kratz
examines a ninth-century parody, De quodam verbece a cane decepto, replete
with mock epic narrative, p/anctus for the fallen ram, and an epitaph. Vergilian and Biblical allusions merge effectively and amusingly. Spiecker's study
examines the influence of Vergil, Lucretius, and Ovid on Thomson's celebrated poem. Thompson's scholarly study relates the Italian masterpiece to
ancient and mediaeval interpretations of the Odyssey and Aeneid. Dante's
depiction of Ulysses reflects his own spiritual autobiography but the poet
transforms his character into that of Aeneas, an anti-Ulysses.
McMaster University
A. G. McKay
