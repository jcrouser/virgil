Crabbe is concerned with Vergil's use of the Theseus and Ariadne theme in
the account of the Orpheus and Eurydice. The article examines compositional
technique in the Aristaeus episode as a whole. Sutton provides a conjectural
account of the origin of the Cacus myth. He suggests that the inspiration
rested in Euripides' satyric drama Sisyphus (or something comparable) as
adapted by Pomponius, the Republican playwright of Sullan times and master
of literary Atellana.
