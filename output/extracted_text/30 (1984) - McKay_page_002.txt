VERGILIAN BIBLIOGRAPHY
1983-84
The present survey of Vergilian scholarship is the twenty-second in the
Vergilius series. Classical World 68:1 (1974), published in association with
The Vergilian Society, Inc., contains my 'Recent Work on Vergil: A Bibliographical Survey, 1964-73.' The survey was reprinted in The Classical World
Bibliography of Vergil (Walter Donlan, ed.), Garland Publishing, Inc. (New
York & London 1978). Subsequent reviews have appeared in Vergilius 19
(1973), 20 (1974), 21 (1975), 22 (1976), 23 (1977), 24 (1978), 25 (1979),
26 (1980), 27 (1981), 28 (1982), and 29 (1983).
This compilation is indebted to the assistance and cooperation of Mills
Memorial Library of McMaster University, the Robarts Library of the University of Toronto, the Library of the British School at Rome, and the Library
of the Union Académique Internationale, Brussels. Offprints of articles and
titles for inclusion in the annual Vergilius review were generously provided by
readers, and scholars are once again invited to send material and suggestions
to the editor of Vergilian Bibliography, c/o Department of Classics, McMaster
University, Hamilton, Ontario, Canada L8S 4M2.

Another exhibition catalogue has surfaced from the bimillenary celebrations in Italy and the Catania march-past of scholarly reflections on Vergil's
Nachleben and the like adds further lustre to the poet's fame. Czechoslovakia
marked the event with addresses appearing in Listy Filologické 106 (1983)
and in Volume III of the Concilium Eirene 16 (1983); the Portuguese have
offered a comparable tribute. Colloquia held in Mantua, Rome and Naples
have yet to appear in print.
