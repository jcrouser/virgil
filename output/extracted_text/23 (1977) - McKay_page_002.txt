Boyle (3) offers an English verse translation of the Bucolics with exegetic
notes and a factual and interpretative introduction suitable for the student of
pastoral who has little or no knowledge of Latin. The text, edited and punctuated by Boyle, contains six different readings from Mynors'OCT (1972).
Coleman (4) provides the text, with apparatus. Exegetical and critical commentary treats points of special interest with due attention to interpretation
of contested passages. An introduction reviews the chronology and arrangement of the poems and treats the earlier history of the pastoral tradition with
admirable compression. Coleman's excellent edition is the first to appear in
English since the end of the 19th century. Austin's posthumous edition of
Aeneid VI (a successor to Sir Frank Fletcher's edition, 1941) will be reviewed
subsequently in Vergilius. Rubén Bonifaz (6) offers a thematic commentary
on Aeneid l-IV
with citations in Spanish and Latin. A. Diez Escanciano's
article provides a translation with notes. Gransden's edition of the 'Augustan
heart of the poem' provides a splendid introduction and detailed commentary
