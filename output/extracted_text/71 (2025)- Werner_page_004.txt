Bonmatí reproduces the Latin text of R. A. B. Mynors with a facing
translation into Spanish, along with a prologue and notes. Cairolli
presents poetic translations into Portuguese, with brief commentary, of
passages from Vergil (Aen. 11.648–69), Horace (Ep. 4 and Carm. 1.37),
Phaedrus (3.1), and Martial (1.2). The main aesthetic concern was to
adapt the Latin original to rhyme, a stylistic resource fundamental to
Portuguese versification. Cristóbal López offers a Latin text of Aen. 6
with a facing translation into Spanish hexameters. Fo critiques various
solutions adopted by Alfonso Traina in his translations of complex
concepts such as fides and pius/pietas in Catullus (Carm. 76), Horace
(Carm. 2.14.2; 4.7.14–16 and 23–24), and Vergil. Hadbawnik’s translation
of the Aeneid previously appeared in two illustrated volumes, and is here
offered in one volume in a more affordable edition. Río Torres-Murciano
