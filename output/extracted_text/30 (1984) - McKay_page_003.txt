Cheney's assessment
of Day Lewis's Aeneid is most favorable. Estevez
finds that Mantinband (1964)
is more faithful to repetitions than Copley
(1975) or Mandelbaum (1971). Steiner's review of Fitzgerald's Aeneid is
masterly; and Breslin's reflections on war, imperialism and the like in his
assessment of Fitzgerald's version is engaging. John Quincy Adams's review of
Sotheby's translation is an arresting museum piece.
