Castagnoli provides a tidy conspectus of the Aeneas legend in Latium in
association with recent archaeological discoveries. Hall's article studies Phocas' 5th century title of Vates Etruscus for Vergil. He examines the Etruscan
background of Mantua, Vergil's cognomen, the Etruscan origins of the Aeneas
myth, and Etruscan claims of descent from Trojan forefathers. Hall argues
that Vergil constructed his Trojan-Etruscan alliance to reflect Etruscan support for Augustus, and to provide mythological precedent for an Italy where
Trojans (Romans) and Etruscans
work together to ensure a well-ordered
society.
