Courtney (34) attacks some textual cruces in the Culex , Copa ,
Elegiae in Maecenatem, Ciris, Catalepton (1, and 14), and Moretum .
Colin Hardie's Oxford Text (35) has an excellent introduction to
the Lives. Akbar Khan (36) argues that Horace in Odes /, 24 refers to an elegy which Vergil had written on the death of Quintilius, an elegy which was careless of moral propriety. Horace's rejoinder is a cautionary poem, with a lesson by example, arguing
that even in the case of an inestimable friend's death, pudor and
modus are the proper principles to observe. Wilhelmina Jashemsky's
article (37) offers valuable illustration for the Copa in the shape
of a Pompei an tavern and garden.
