VERGILIAN BIBLIOGRAPHY XIX
1980-81
The present survey of Vergilian scholarship is the nineteenth inthe Vergilius series. Classical
World 68:1
(1974), published in association with the
Vergilian
Society,
Inc.,
contains
my
'Recent Work on Vergil:
A Bibliographical Survey, 1964-73.' The survey was reprinted in The Classical World
Bibliography of Vergil (Walter Donlan, ed.), Garland Publishing, Inc. (New
York & London 1978). Subsequent surveys have appeared in Vergilius 19
(1973), 20 (1974), 21 (1975), 22 (1976), 23 (1977), 24 (1978), 25 (1979)
and 26 (1980).
Several noteworthy collections are contained inthe 1980-81 Bibliography.
Arethusa marked the bimillenium of Vergil's death with a special issue containing an Introduction by M. C. J. Putnam (7-15), a modern bibliography
(179-86), and eleven articles. Aufsteig und Niedergang der römischen
Welt
(Berlin 1979, 1980) made its impressive bibliographies available toan appreciative readership during the year. Special celebrations of the Bimillenium in
Italy (19-24 September 1981), the Vergilian Society's colloquium at Cumae
(19-23 July 1982), and other conventions inMainz and Prague (to mention
only a few) promise to release cascades of appreciation for the Master Poet
during the ensuing year.
Gratitude is owing to the Mills Memorial Library of McMaster University,
to the Robarts Library of the University of Toronto, to the Firestone Library
of Princeton University, and to the collections and courtesies of the Library
of the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton. Offprints of articles and
bibliographic
notices
for
inclusion in the
annual
Vergilius
review arrive
throughout the year as a largesse which isenormously welcome and helpful.
