Vuković notes that Vergil has Evander trace the origins of the name of
the River Tiber back to the death of a giant called Thybris (Aen. 8.330–
332). The reference to the violent (asper, 330) giant can be understood
as etymological wordplay on the Greek word ὕβρις and as a potential
allusion to the grammatical debate on the nature of aspiration. Varro’s De
gente populi Romani is identified as a source for the characterization of the
Tiber as a giant in primeval times. Ziosi remarks that the flame imagery
in Aen. 2 and 4, through intertextual references particularly to Euripides’s
tragedies relating to the Trojan cycle, allows us to see the fall of Troy as
foreshadowing in myth the destruction of Carthage.
