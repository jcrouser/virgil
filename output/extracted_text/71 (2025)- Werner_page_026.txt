Gartner reads the Poros scene in the Alexandreis, a Latin epic poem
composed by the theologian Walter of Châtillon (ca. 1135–1201) on the
exploits of Alexander the Great, as imitatio et aemulatio of the final scene
of the Aeneid. Aristotle’s speech in that poem also alludes to the end of
the Aeneid, as well as to Anchises’s speech in Aen. 6. Gibson argues that
the twelfth-century monk Iohannes de Alta Silva’s Dolopathos, a prose
treatment of the Seven Sages, is the earliest extant witness to the legend
that Vergil and the apostle Paul narrowly missed meeting each other in
Sicily. Gredler writes about the medieval legend of Vergil in the basket.
