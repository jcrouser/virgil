1-3 are fully developed in the Aristaeus epyllion. Hardie concentrates on the underworld theme to which Vergil's mind reverts over and over in preparation for the final
full treatment Hardie notes, reasonably, that such preparation for Orpheus could not
have prefaced the laudes Galli. Finally Hardie counters and expands earlier 'political',
'moral', and 'mystical' interpretations of the Aristaeus epyllion with a poetical explanation. White's study of Roman farming (22) is comprehensive and detailed, and invaluable for students of Vergil's agricultural lore.
