20. Roberto De Simone, Il Segno di Virgilio (Pozzuoli 1982).
Bauza's collection of papers to mark the bimillenary signals South American enthusiasm for the poet. Cottrill's notice provides a list of exhibitions of
Vergiliana in the U. K., a valuable guide for visitors. The Strasbourg exhibition catalogue features Grüninger's woodcuts that appeared during the 16th
century. Pope John Paul II's oration to the members of the Latinitas Foundation (30/11/81) will appeal to Latin classes. Petro Magno's volume offers a
tidy perspective of present-day scholarship on many aspects of Vergil's opera
and includes a helpful bibliography (205-19). Melone forecasts the contents
of the Atti of the 'Convegno mondiale' in Mantua, Rome, and Naples during
1981. Nutting's song for the Latin class was composed for the earlier Bimillennium. Oroz Reta offers details on Vergilian observances in Spain. Pasqualetti's enthusiasm is manifest in 24 alcaic stanzas. Polara provides a precis of
the commemorative lectures on Vergil's Eclogues in Naples (1981). Rome's
reporters interviewed M. C. J.Putnam after his commemorative lecture with
startling headlines .Uno studioso americano attaca il mito deVeroe troiano.
Putnam's contribution to the conference was entitled 'The Hesitation of
Aeneas.' Schilling gives a global view of the poet at each stage of his development. Roberto De Simone, in an impressive format, expands on the mediaeval
Vergil and the Neapolitan legends with a marvelous array of Grüninger woodcuts.
