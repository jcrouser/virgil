VERGILIAN BIBLIOGRAPHY (1977-78)
The present survey of Vergilian scholarship is the sixteenth inthe Vergilius
series. Classical World 68.1 (1974), published in association with The Vergilian Society, Inc., contains my 'Recent Work on Vergil: A Bibliographical
Survey, 1964-1973.' The 92-page
survey
may be ordered either from the
offices of The Classical World or from the Treasurer of The Vergilian Society,
Inc. The same survey has been reprinted, along with earlier surveys compiled
by George E. Duckworth, in The Classical World Bibliography of Vergil (ed.
Walter Donlan), Garland Publishing, Inc., New York & London, 1978. Subsequent surveys have appeared in Vergilius 19 (1973), 20 (1974), 21 1975), 22
(1976), and 23 (1977).
The editor of 'Vergilian Bibliography' welcomes reprints of articles and
bibliographical
notices for inclusion in the annual
review. Kindly address
correspondence to Professor A. G. McKay, Department of Classics, McMaster
University, Hamilton, Ontario, Canada L8S 4M2.

Bernstein studies an interesting museum piece. Collard's sympathetic profile sheds
light on
an exceptionally
fine editor of Vergil's texts. Perret's
edition and translation of Aeneid 1-8 lacks lustre and falters alongside Geymonat's and Mynors' editions. Stégen offers a school text, as does Muir. The
latter helps to enrich the growing Cambridge Latin Texts which have invaded
the Secondary Schools of the English-speaking world.
