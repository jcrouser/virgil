225. Edward L. Harrison, lAeneid 9.240,' Vergilius 28 (1982) 62.
Green responds to Cressey (LCM 6: 5 [May 1981] ) who interpreted A en.
1.455 (operumque laborem) as 'the work of navvies'. Green's article advances
Ausonius, Mosella, 305-7, where operumque labores is used and hardly admits
Cressey's interpretation. Green suggests a variant reading for A en. 'A55-artificumque manus interne (Mynors, inter se) operumque laborem. Interne suggests suppressed wonder; inter se, or intra se, suggests something more emotional. Harrison offers a new reading of Aen. 9.240, si fortuna permittitis usi
(Mynors, uti), and translates "if, exploiting fortune, you send us through to
seek Aeneas and the walls of Pallanteum ..."
