VERGILIAN BIBLIOGRAPHY
1981-82
The present survey of Vergilian scholarship is the twentieth in the Vergilius series. Classical
World 68:1
(1974), published in association with the
Vergilian
Society,
Inc.,
contains
my 'Recent Work on Vergil: A Bibliographical Survey, 1964-73/ The survey was reprinted in The Classical World
Bibliography of Vergil (Walter Donlan, ed.), Garland Publishing, Inc. (New
York and London 1978). Subsequent reviews have appeared in Vergilius 19
(1973), 20 (1974), 21 (1975), 22 (1976), 23 (1977), 24 (1978), 25 (1979),
26 (1980), and 27 (1981).
The Vergilian Bimillenium (19 B.C. - A.D. 1982) has excited scholars to
renewed enthusiasm for the Master Poet. Special symposia and colloquia have
celebrated the event
in Europe, the United Kingdom, and North America
with generous outpourings of articles and addresses in their train. We await
the publication of proceedings of several gatherings in Mainz, Prague, Mantua,
Rome, Naples, and Cumae (The Vergilian Society's event).
The present compilation owes much to the assistance and cooperation of
the Mills Memorial Library of McMaster University, to the Robarts Library of
the University of Toronto, to the library of the American Academy in Rome,
and to the Carolinum
Library
in
Prague. Offprints of articles and bibliographic notices for inclusion in the annual Vergilius review are welcomed with
enthusiasm and scholars are asked to send material to the editor of Vergilian
Bibliography, c/o Department of Classics, McMaster University, Hamilton,
Ontario, Canada L8S 4M2.
