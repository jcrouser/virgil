R. D. Williams, T. S. Pattie,
Virgil: His Poetry through the Ages
(London 1982)
[in connection with the British Library Exhibition, 17 September, 198227 February, 1983].
The exhibition catalogue (198) illustrates Vergil's role in European art and
cultural history: La fortuna di Virgilio attraverso le opere a stampa (9-24); La
fortuna di Virgilio nella cultura Europea (25-62); La fortuna di Virgilio ne/I'
arte (63-262), with lavish illustrations; and Bibliography (263-8). The catalogue provides splendid support for studies in Vergilian influence in several
realms. Kaster studies Vergilian references in Macrobius' Saturnalia. Perkins
and Reeves shed fresh light on T. S. Eliot's ties with Vergil. Draheim's invaluable study of Vergilian influence on musical compositions from the Baroque
period
to

works
by

Ives, Malipiero, Penderecki, Stravinsky,
Richard Strauss,
Liszt,
the
incomparable
Mozart,
Schubert,
and
others (30-32; 220-225;
261-2).
R. D. Williams and T. S. Pattie (Assistant Keeper, Department of
Manuscripts of the British Library) have collaborated to produce a critical
reassessment of Vergil's poetry, with studies of the manuscript tradition, and
of the immense influence of his poetry on succeeding generations of Latin
and English poets, and on the popular imagination. Williams provides Part I:
Virgil and his poetry; the poems; and Virgil today. A selective bibliography
appears in the volume and appendices include details of Latin mss of Vergil in
the British Library.
