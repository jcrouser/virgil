VERGILIUS
Clark offers a searching analysis of Aen. 6, 739-51, lines which recall
Pindar, Empedocles, the Gold Leaves, and Plato, explores the imagery of the
wheel, clarifies punctuation, and counters R. D. Williams, who construes the
lines mistakenly.

Coleiro favours Skutsch's schema for Bucolic IV (HSCP13 (Í96§) 153-69)
and postulates a missing line after verse 23; he supplies, with the assistance of
Georgics 3, 537, the following: nec lupus insidias componet oviiia circum.
Monat uses an
indirect tradition
to establish ferrea progenies (cf. Hesiod,
sidereion genos). Kollmann reads at Aen. 12, 709: coisse viros et decernere
ferro.
