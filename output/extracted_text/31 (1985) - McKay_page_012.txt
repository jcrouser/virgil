Basson's treatment of Mezentius is a companion piece for Gotoff's more
venturesome study of the 'paradoxical character* of the Etruscan exile. Gotoff concentrates on Vergilian invention, and on the scenes, vignettes, and
nuances that affect the character of the antagonistic hero and the culminating
event. Harrison's Sussex lecture studies Juno's hostility to Rome and its
extension after the reconciliation of A. 12. Vergil keeps Juno ignorant of the
ultimate ruin of Carthage by the Romans; she does not have access to the
prophecies of Anchises, and her efforts to save her beloved Carthage are
thwarted by her ignorance.
