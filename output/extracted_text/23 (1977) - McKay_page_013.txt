Alfonsi studies topoi in the Greek theatre and their appearance inRoman
writings. He instances poverty as the teacher of the technai inAristophanes'
P/outos and Vergil, Georgics I, 121ff. Parthenius was Vergil's tutor inGreek
and also compiled a handbook of erotic tales for Cornelius Gallus. Vergil,
according to Clausen, imitated Parthenius' verses inG. I, 437, and Aen. IV,
645-51. McLennan, pace Snell, argues that Vergil hardly promoted the status
of Arcadia much further than its position inextant Hellenistic bucolic settings. He also notes that a recent bucolic papyrus (Page, GLP i. 123) provides
