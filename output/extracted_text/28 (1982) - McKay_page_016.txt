Gaebel shows that the relationship of two manuscripts, Vatican Palatinus
lat. 1631 (P) and Guelferbytanus Gudianus lat. 2° 70 (gamma) is much less
close than has been assumed by Ribbeck, Sabbadini, Mynors, and Geymonat.
Gaebel examines four manuscripts for Bucolics 1-3, including Vat. lat. 3867
(R) of the fourth century, and Bernensis 172(a) of the ninth century. Georgics 4.1-225
is
included
in the examination
with
important conclusions.
Courtney's article, after examining numerous instances of textual problems
remarks that there is a need for frank acknowledgment of the existence of
genuine difficulties in Vergil's text which require close study and a search for
answers. 'These solutions . . . have in some cases come from scholars, such as
Peerlkamp, whose names are hardly vogue words; but the fact that such
scholars have made dozens of unwarranted suggestions does not absolve us
from impartial consideration and recognition of their successes' (28).
