Eleanor Leach's interpretation of the political Eclogues of Calpurnius Siculus contains useful direction and corrective for critics of Vergil's pastorals.
Hudson-Williams (68) detects imitations of Vergil inValerius Flaccus and a
measure of felicity in the art. Bews (69) shows how Tacitus drew on Vergil
for literary associations. Although the strain of imperial optimism inVergil's
epic is countered by the imperial pessimism of the Annals,Tacitus nevertheless finds inspiration and perspective as literary historian inVergil's Augustan
epic. The Vergilian language which pervades Tacitus' accounts of Tiberius and
Germanicus lends dimension and depth to the historian's interpretation of his
material. Baldwin (70) combs Gellius for critical comments on Vergil's style.
