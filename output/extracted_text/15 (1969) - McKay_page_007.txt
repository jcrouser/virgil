The Oxford text of the Appendix Vergiliana (1966) has helped to revive interest in
these problematic poems. Doeblin's review (56) is useful and incisive. Courtney (54)
changes 'hora' to 'icta' ('overthrown'), and Wistrand (60) emphasizes the dilemmas
attaching to the Third Catalepton. Murgia's critical notes (58) are invaluable to the
student of Aeneid 3-5.
