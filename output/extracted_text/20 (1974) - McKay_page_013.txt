Eric Sjöqvist, Sicily and the Greeks.
Ann Arbor: The University of Michigan
Press, 1973. Pp. xiii, 90. $10.00.
In this stimulating volume, originally presented as the Ninth Series of the
Jerome Lectures, Eric Sjöqvist examines the historical process resulting inthe
Hellenization of the indigenous population of Sicily and the concomitant problem of the degree to which Greek Civilization in Sicily was modified by the
native element.
An introductory chapter stresses the casual and tangential
nature of Bronze Age contacts between the Aegean world and Sicily. The
permanent Hellenic occupation of the island, beginning with the great eighth
century colonization, forms the central portion of the work.
