Fasciano's study of numen treats it as an attribute of divinity, and as
synonymous with deus. Quiter's monograph on the implications of the rituals
attaching to the oracular cult marks important advances in understanding.
Schoder's study of Vergil's vision of life is characteristically perceptive and
persuasive. Wiseman emphasizes the importance of Cybele in the Aeneid; her
temple was close to Augustus' own complex, and her treatment by Vergil is in
keeping with the rehabilitation of the Magna Mater. Despite her orgiastic cult,
she is represented as protector of Aeneas and as guarantor of his victory.
