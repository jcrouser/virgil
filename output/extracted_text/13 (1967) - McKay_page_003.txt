is associated, by Savage, with the Greek Pleiades, as is his
mother Magia-Maia. Segal (8) finds different attitudes to nature in
both Aristaeus and Orpheus who 'frame' the essentially tragic life
of the bees. There is, he feels, a thematic contrast of man and the
bees, and there is also a stylistic contrast between the first and
second half of the book, between a Lucretian and a more Homeric,
more characteristically Vergilian half. And there is a more fundamental and revealing contrast between the 'real' world of bees and
the 'ideal' world of myth and poetry, which unlike the former, is
filled with death and disaster.
