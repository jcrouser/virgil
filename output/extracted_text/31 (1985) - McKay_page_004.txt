27. James R. G. Wright, 'Virgil's Pastoral Programme: Theocritus, Callimachus, and Eclogue 1,' PCPS 209 (1983) 107-58.
Filimon's article provides a German summary for those who do not read
Romanian. Havas makes yet another vain effort to detect political realities
within the Bucolics. Kenney's helpful article treats Eclogues 2 and 8 with
attention to their elegiac character and their relevance to the process of
literary creation.
Wright detects a programme of pastoral poetry which is
specifically Roman with indebtedness to Theocritus, Callimachus and Hesiod.
