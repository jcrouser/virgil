Trevizam makes a lexical comparison of passages that refer to agricultural
implements and to the location of beehives from two types of expository
texts: Palladius’s Opus agriculturae, a technical treatise; and Vergil’s
Georgics, a work of didactic poetry. The results point to the prevalence of
the function of docere in Palladius and of delectare in Vergil.

Breed offers a geocritical analysis of the plague landscape and related
spaces in G. 3 to illuminate the generic trajectories of Vergil’s poem.
G. 1.436–37, Freer writes, is modeled on a verse by Parthenius of Nicaea
(fr. 36 Lightfoot). A likely further allusion is to an epigram by Philodemus,
AP 6.349 (= 19 Gow-Page = 34 Sider). This allusion may have an Epicurean
resonance.
